How It's Calculated

Monthly Mortgage: Loan × [r(1+r)ⁿ] ÷ [(1+r)ⁿ-1] where r = monthly rate, n = total payments
Buying Net Worth: Home Value × (1 + Appreciation Rate)ⁿ - Loan Balance - 6% Selling Costs
Renting Net Worth: Down Payment invested + Monthly Savings Differential, compounding at investment return rate
Key Assumption: Renter invests down payment amount and difference between monthly buy vs rent costs

Key Considerations

What the numbers don't capture

Flexibility

Renting offers easy relocation for career opportunities. Buying ties you to a location but builds forced savings through home equity.

Market Timing

Housing markets are cyclical. Buying at peak prices or high interest rates can significantly impact returns. Be patient.

Opportunity Cost

Down payment money could compound in the stock market. This calculator accounts for that, but many analyses don't.

Lifestyle Factors

Emotional satisfaction, stability for kids, ability to customize - these matter but can't be quantified in a calculator.
