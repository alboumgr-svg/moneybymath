How It's Calculated

Standard Deduction: Subtracted from gross income before any tax is applied. 2026 amounts: $16,100 (Single), $32,200 (Married Filing Jointly), $24,150 (Head of Household).
Progressive Brackets: Only the income within each bracket is taxed at that rate. Earning $60,000 as a single filer does not mean all $60,000 is taxed at 22% - only the slice of income between $12,400–$50,400 is taxed at 22%.
Effective Rate: Total federal tax ÷ Gross income. Almost always lower than your marginal (top bracket) rate because lower brackets cover the first dollars of income.
Marginal Rate: The rate that applies to your last dollar of income. Useful for evaluating raises, freelance income, or deductions.

Tax brackets shown are 2026 IRS figures (IRS Revenue Procedure 2025-32). Brackets adjust annually for inflation. This tool does not include state taxes, FICA, or any credits or deductions beyond the standard deduction.
